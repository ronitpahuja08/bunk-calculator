FAQ (the stuff everyone asks)

How is attendance percentage calculated?

Simple division, nothing fancy: (classes attended ÷ total classes held) × 100. If you've been to 42 out of 56 classes, that's 42/56 × 100 = 75%. Your college isn't hiding some secret formula — it's just math.

How many classes can I bunk with 75% attendance?

Depends entirely on your total classes so far — that's exactly what this calculator works out for you. The more classes already held, the more "buffer" you've usually built up, since each future bunk adds to both attended and total in a way that drags your % down. Plug in your numbers above and the tool does the math instantly.

What happens if attendance falls below 75%?

Varies by institution, but common outcomes include being barred from sitting exams, getting "detained" for the term, needing to reappear, or facing a fine. Some colleges give a grace window before penalties kick in. Bottom line: check your own institution's rulebook, because "75%" isn't a universal law of physics.

Does medical leave count (condonation)?

Often yes, partially — many colleges allow "condonation" where documented medical leave (with a certificate) can be excused or counted separately, sometimes bumping your effective requirement down a few percent. But policies differ a lot between institutions, so this calculator can't account for it automatically. Always double-check with your department or attendance office before assuming you're covered.
